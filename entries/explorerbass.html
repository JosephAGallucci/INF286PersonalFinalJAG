Leo Jaymz Explorer DIY Bass Kit

Guitar Specifications/History

Amazon quality at its finest. This bass came together from a DIY kit that was put together over the course of a few weeks. It still has all of its stock (probably cheap)
hardware, such as the no-soldering electronics, except for the bridge. The kit contained a three-point bridge, a much maligned design that I did not want to even bother with. As a result, the body was modified to accept
a Fender style bridge. Originally, I put on a Gotoh 201B bridge, but a little over a year later that got replaced with a heavier Fender Hi-Mass bridge in a vain attempt
to make the body heavier. This was done to help counteract its neck dive, a design problem where the body is lighter relative to the neck, causing the guitar/bass to fall forward when not constantly held up.
This particular kit is not available anymore but you can still buy something very similar to it today.

Technical Details

  • Mahogany Body
  • Maple Neck
  • Unknown Fingerboard
  • Blue Stain Finish
  • Red Stain Headstock
  • Stock Snap-Connector Pickups
  • Fender Hi-Mass Bridge

Personal History

My Squier broke at the output jack. Once it was fixed up, I had begun to devlop a desire to learn how a bass/guitar is made, mostly so I could get
closer to doing my own maintenance. Come Christmastime, and I ask for this bass DIY kit. I then recieve this kit and build it over the course of about 1-2 weeks.
This bass is far from a great player, it's got this very big neck which truthfully is not a deal breaker, but certianly not as playable as my othere guitars. However, this bass suffers from neck dive, an
uncomfortable body shape, plus other things like the finnicky input jack output that broke on me. As a result, despite liking how this bass sounds I don't find myself
playing it for more than a few minutes at a time.
