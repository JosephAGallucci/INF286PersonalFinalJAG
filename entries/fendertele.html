Fender 1999 American Fat Telecaster

Guitar Specifications/Details

This is a Fender American Fat Telecaster built in 1999. The serial number indicates that it was made in 1998 but I did a serial number lookup
from Fender and it came back as being made in February 1999. The name "Fat" derives from its pickup configuration: the bridge pickup is the same
as a regular Telecaster, but the neck pickup is a "DH-1", which is a humbucker Fender was making around this time in the late 90s-early 00s. Regular
Telecasters have a single coil where this big humbucker is. To adjust to this, the switch on the guitar has five positions instead of three to account
for the extra sounds this model has.

Technical Details

  • Alder Body
  • Maple Neck
  • Rosewood Fingerboard
  • Candy Apple Red Finish
  • American Standard Bridge Pickup
  • DH-1 Neck Pickup
Personal History Upon starting guitar again, I had begun to develop this taste for Fenders. Then my grandfather hits me with this very guitar, shows it to me via
pictures... then proceeds to give a Made in America Fender when I had been playing for mere months. It had already gone through
at least 2 owners, as he mentioned purchasing it in 2005. To this day that still feels wrong given my playing ability at the time and the value of an American Fender.
However I played this guitar bascially exclusively from the moment I got it until my Epiphones got resurrected. Teles are viewed as country guitars
but it handles rock, punk and even metal like a champ. It's much snappier, twangier sound gives a slightly harder edge to the notes than a Les Paul does.
