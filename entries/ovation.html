Ovation Matrix 1132

Guitar Specifications


This is my primary acoustic guitar. It's not strictly an Ovation, rather being branded
under the "Matrix" name. However the information inside the sound hole states the company of manufacture:
Kaman Music, who also owned Ovation. As such, this guitar shares many of Ovation's construction traits like
the non-wood "bowl back" combined with the wood top and the aluminum/resin neck as opposed to a wooden neck. It does not, however, have a
pickup inside. This guitar's serial number dates to 1972, after an initial misunderstanding that it was made in 1982.

Technical Details

  • Composite Body/Wood Top
  • Aluminum/Resin Neck
  • Rosewood Fingerboard
  • Sunburst Finish

Personal Experience

This is a guitar I got from my grandfather, as part of collection liquidation. It's a bit smaller of a body shape, making
it a bit more comfortable for me as someone who mainly plays electric. Sometimes, regular acoustic guitars can feel a little bit
deep and uncomfortable on my body but this guitar feels much better in that regard. E Standard is also unattainable now because on its most recent string change, the A string tuner gets
effectively stuck after tuning it enough. This guitar is mainly kept in E flat as a result but I like E flat so it's not a super big deal to me.
