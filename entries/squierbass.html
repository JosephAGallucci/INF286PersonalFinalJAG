2021 Squier Classic Vibe 50s Precision Bass

Guitar Specifications/History


This is a Sweetwater floor model Squier Classic Vibe 50's Precision Bass, based off of a 1956 Precision Bass. This means that while the body is contoured, it still has
the Tele-style single coil rather than the later style split-coil pickup. It's output jack broke in August 2022 out of seemingly nowhere, and had to get fixed up.
Around mid 2023, I decided to substantially modify this bass in order to keep up with my nicer bass and to address a few issues that had started to creep up with age and
admittetly aggressive playstyle. First, the nut was swapped out for a TUSQ XL to address a playbility issue. Then, I changed the original bridge which had
started to wear down on the saddles to a Gotoh 201B. Next, I changed the original pickup out for a Seymour Duncan Quarter Pound, before finally changing out the stock white
pickguard to a black pickguard.

Technical Details

  • Pine Body
  • Maple Neck & Fingerboard
  • 2-Tone Sunburst Finish
  • Seymour Duncan Quarter Pound Pickup
  • Gotoh 201B Bridge

Personal History

I bought this bass on a family vacation about a week after I got the EB-3. At the time, I had family live near Fort Wayne where the Sweetwater campus is. So, we went to Sweetwater and came out
of the store with this bass. From there, it became my main instrument and is what I consider my formative bass. This is the bass that I brought to a Sweetwater Rock Camp and
played my first actual show on. It has also done quite a few recordings for both class projects and personal projects, pre-and-post modification.
