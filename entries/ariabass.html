1971 Aria EB-3 Copy

Guitar Specifications/History

The exact year of this bass is unknown, but given the background info I know on this bass, this is roughly from about 1972. As mentioned, this
is a copy of the Gibson EB-3 bass, made in Japan during what is these days referred to as the "lawsuit era", where Japanese companies went rampant
with making copies of American guitars, which by the end of the 70s became on par/better than the American counterparts. Unlike a Gibson, this bass
has a bolt-on neck and not the glued in neck of the Gibson.

Technical Details

  • Mahogany Body & Neck
  • Rosewood Fingerboard
  • Cherry Red Finish
  • Stock Electronics

Personal History

This is yet another instrument courtesy of my grandfather. He was the original owner, and mentioned how he had bought this bass after falling into the
scenario of "too many guitar players, someone needs to play bass". From there, this bass just sat with him until I was given it... where its flatwound strings
were instantly yanked off. I had this bass for a week until I bought my Squier but has always remained in the collection. It was this bass that got me into my
first actual band, but only because my Squier broke on the output jack and this was the other bass that I had. It's tone doesn't really fit a lot of what I do
but it's a novel sound nonetheless, and one with quite a bit of family history.
